### Fee context (facts only)

**Scenario:** Mutual fund exit load

**Last checked:** 2026-03-22

- Exit load is a fee that may apply when an investor redeems units of a mutual fund scheme before a stated period from allotment, where the scheme’s offer document permits it.
- SEBI-regulated mutual funds disclose exit load structure, if any, in the scheme information document and addendum; terms vary by scheme category and are not uniform across all funds.
- Exit load, when charged, is typically a percentage of the applicable net asset value and is retained within the scheme for the benefit of remaining unit holders, as described in scheme documents.
- Not all mutual fund schemes levy an exit load; index funds and some liquid schemes may specify nil exit load subject to the current offer document.
- Investors can verify the applicable load for a specific scheme from the latest SID/KIM and factsheet published by the AMC.
- Charges other than exit load (e.g. stamp duty, statutory levies) may apply as disclosed separately in scheme documents.
